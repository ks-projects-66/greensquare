The evidence

Same case. Same data. Same day. The framework changed the answer.

We gave the same business case to a capable model twice: once on its own, once running the GreenSquare framework. The recommendation reversed.

Without the framework

“Launch the product.”

A competent, staged launch plan. It answered the question it was asked, and never tested whether launching was the right use of the money.

With the framework

“Do not launch yet.”

Run an A$450k diagnostic first, to test the launch against fixing two underperforming business units sitting beneath the new one.

The framework noticed the launch was not the real decision, and surfaced the harder one the brief was avoiding.

Same reversal on Claude, GPT and Gemini, tested independently.

What happened

The launch was masking the real decision.

A chief executive was weighing whether to launch a new product line. Asked plainly, a capable model did what it was asked: it produced a sensible, staged launch plan. The thinking was competent. The framing went unquestioned.

Running the same case through the framework, the model interviewed the situation before answering. It found two underperforming business units sitting beneath the proposed launch, and recognised that committing to launch would paper over them. The recommendation changed: do not launch yet, and spend A$450k on a diagnostic that tests the launch against fixing what is already there.

The numbers did not change. The data did not change. What changed was whether the model questioned the decision it was handed. That is the whole point of the framework.

The company in this case is fictional. The reversal is a real benchmark result, reproduced independently on Claude, GPT and Gemini.
