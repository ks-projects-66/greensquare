The evidence

Same case. Same data. Different decision.

We ran the same business case twice: once as a baseline, once with the GreenSquare framework. The baseline answered the brief. The framework tested the brief.

Without the framework

“Launch the product.”

A competent staged launch plan. It accepted the surface decision and focused on execution.

With the framework

“Do not launch yet.”

Run an A$450k diagnostic first, to test the launch against fixing two underperforming business units.

The framework found the decision under the decision. The launch was masking the harder call.

The same reversal was reproduced on Claude, GPT and Gemini in the benchmark.

What happened

The launch brief was too narrow.

A chief executive was weighing whether to launch a new product line. The baseline run produced a sensible staged launch plan. The thinking was competent. The framing went untested.

The GreenSquare run identified two underperforming business units sitting beneath the proposed launch, and recognised that a launch commitment could hide the real trade-off. The recommendation changed: do not launch yet, and spend A$450k on a diagnostic that tests the launch against fixing what is already there.

The numbers did not change. The data did not change. What changed was whether the decision itself was tested before the answer was written.

The company in this case is fictional. The reversal is a real benchmark result, reproduced independently on Claude, GPT and Gemini.
