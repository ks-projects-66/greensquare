Evidence

Same case. Same data. Different decision.

The benchmark compares the same decision case with and without the GreenSquare framework. The difference is not the data. It is the structure of the thinking.

The test

One decision, run two ways, on three models.

A fictional Sydney manufacturer (FreshTaste Foods, about A$60m in revenue) weighing whether to launch a new product line under tight cash, capacity and covenant constraints. The same case and the same inputs were given to Claude, ChatGPT and Gemini, each in two conditions: a baseline answer, and the same model run through the GreenSquare framework. Every output was scored against one twelve-dimension rubric.

This is the case the product is built for. It is the calibration example inside the Decision Brief: find the decision under the stated one, and have the nerve to name it.

Baseline

Launch the product.

A capable plan that accepted the surface question.

GreenSquare

Do not launch yet.

A stronger call that tested whether launch was the right decision at all.

The framework found the decision under the decision.

Result

Better structure produced a more decision-ready answer.

The GreenSquare run pushed the model to test the frame, compare alternatives and expose the assumptions behind the recommendation. The reversal is the useful finding.

The company in the case is fictional. The benchmark is useful evidence, not statistical proof.
