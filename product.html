What you get

Five parts, every time.

The output is a clean markdown brief generated inside the chat. It is short enough to use, but structured enough to defend.

The value is not a prettier answer. It is the discipline: the advisor tests whether the stated decision is the real one, marks what is known and unknown, and names what would change the recommendation.

  • The decision, and why now. The real decision as the advisor understands it, with the trigger that makes it live.
  • The options, compared. Not a list. A comparison against two to four criteria that matter for this call.
  • The recommendation. The call, plus the one or two assumptions it rests on, tagged as given, inferred or unknown.
  • The next steps. Three to five concrete actions, sequenced against the clock if timing matters.
  • What would change this call. The specific risks or unknowns that would flip the recommendation.

How it thinks

It does not reward vague input.

01

It asks first

Small reversible calls get a few sharp questions. Higher-stakes decisions get deeper discovery before the brief is written.

02

It challenges the frame

Before producing the brief, it asks whether the stated choice is the real decision or a smaller question standing in front of it.

03

It tags the evidence

Claims that carry weight are marked as given, inferred or unknown, so the recommendation does not hide weak foundations.
